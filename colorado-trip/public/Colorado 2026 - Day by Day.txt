Key Tips for the Whole Trip
Altitude: 2,300–3,700 meters along the entire way. Drink plenty of water from day one, moderate pace for the first two days, watch out for headaches.
Sun: Much stronger than in Georgia – SPF 50 sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses every day, even when it's cool.
Afternoon storms: Expected almost every day in July in the mountains – trails should start early and finish by noon. Do not be above the tree line when there is lightning.
Cold nights: 7–11°C at night even in July. A warm layer for everyone in the daypack – always.
Key gear: Closed shoes (dunes), water shoes (Zapata, rafting, Medano), swimsuits, quick-drying clothes, headlamps for stargazing night.
